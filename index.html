Implementing the Swatch Bharath Mission

Swatch Bharat is an initiative launched by the Indian government in 2014. The campaign is aimed at creating a cleaner and healthier India. The mission is to make India open defecation free by 2nd October 2019.The "Swachh Bharat Abhiyan" or "Clean India Mission" is a nationwide cleanliness campaign initiated by the Government of India in 2014 with the aim of making India cleaner and more hygienic.

Objectives of the Swatch Bharath

To eliminate open defecation in India. To reduce littering and plastic waste. To promote cleanliness and hygiene in public places.
1. Open Defecation Free (ODF) India: One of the primary objectives of the campaign is to eliminate the practice of open defecation across the country. This involves constructing toilets in rural areas, promoting the use of toilets, and creating awareness about the importance of sanitation and hygiene.
2. Sanitation Facilities: The campaign aims to ensure access to adequate and functional sanitation facilities for all individuals, including households, schools, and public places. This involves constructing and maintaining toilets, urinals, and waste management systems.
3. Cleanliness in Public Places: The Swachh Bharat Abhiyan emphasizes the importance of cleanliness in public spaces such as streets, parks, markets, and tourist destinations. The objective is to improve the overall cleanliness and aesthetics of public places and encourage people to maintain cleanliness in their surroundings.
4. Health and Environmental Impact: The campaign aims to improve public health by addressing sanitation-related diseases and reducing pollution. By promoting proper waste management and sanitation practices, it aims to minimize health risks and create a cleaner and healthier environment.
5. Behavioral Change Communication: The Swachh Bharat Abhiyan includes extensive communication and awareness campaigns to engage and educate people about the importance of cleanliness. The objective is to bring about a positive change in attitudes and behaviors towards cleanliness and hygiene.

Strategy to implement Swatch Bharath

Construction of toilets in rural and urban areas. Behavior change and awareness campaigns. Solid waste management and recycling.
Construction of Toilets: One of the primary objectives of the campaign is to eliminate open defecation by constructing toilets in households, schools, and public places. The government has emphasized the need for individual household toilets (IHHL) and community sanitary complexes (CSC) to improve sanitation facilities across the country.
Community Engagement: The campaign actively involves communities, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and other stakeholders at the grassroots level. It encourages people to actively participate in cleanliness drives, create awareness, and collaborate with local authorities to implement cleanliness initiatives effectively.
Generating awareness and bringing about a behavior change regarding sanitation practices through volunteers, known as Swatchhagrahis, or “Ambassadors of cleanliness” .
Implementing community-led total sanitation approach to motivate people to stop open defecation and adopt hygienic habits.
Developing solid and liquid waste management systems at the village level to ensure proper disposal and recycling of waste.
Providing safe and adequate drinking water supply to every person.
